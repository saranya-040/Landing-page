Explore India's Vibrant Festivals

Diwali

Diwali, the festival of lights, symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. It is celebrated with great enthusiasm by lighting oil lamps, bursting fireworks, and exchanging sweets.

Holi

Holi, the festival of colors, marks the arrival of spring and the end of winter. People celebrate by throwing colorful powders and water at each other, dancing, and enjoying festive foods.

Navaratri

Navaratri, celebrated over nine nights, honors the divine feminine goddess. It involves traditional dance performances, fasting, and prayers dedicated to Goddess Durga.

Eid al-Fitr (Ramzan)

Eid al-Fitr, also known as Ramzan or Ramadan, marks the end of the holy month of fasting. It is a time of joyous feasting, giving to charity, and coming together with family and friends.

Onam

Onam is a harvest festival celebrated in Kerala, marked by vibrant cultural performances, elaborate feasts, and the decoration of homes with flower carpets.

Pongal

Pongal is a harvest festival celebrated in Tamil Nadu to express gratitude to the sun god for a bountiful harvest. It involves rituals like boiling rice in pots until they overflow, symbolizing abundance.
